# Bittensor Faces Backlash: Covenant AI and Governance

**Summary:** Bittensor (TAO) drew scrutiny after a public dispute with subnet developer Covenant AI. The episode renewed questions about the protocol’s governance model; TAO’s price moved in line with elevated uncertainty among market participants.

## Covenant AI’s Departure and Allegations

Covenant AI announced its departure from the Bittensor network and accused the protocol of operating under a **centralized governance** structure. Founder **Sam Dare** characterized the system as **“decentralization theatre”** and claimed that meaningful decision-making power is concentrated with Bittensor founder **Jacob Steeves** (also known as Const). Dare argued that this structure made it harder for the team to keep building or raising capital in the ecosystem.

## Governance Context: Triumvirate and Opentensor

The debate focuses on Bittensor’s current governance design, including a transitional **“Triumvirate”** in which members of the **Opentensor Foundation** hold root permissions alongside a senate. Framed as temporary, this arrangement is cited by critics as weakening the network’s decentralization narrative.

## Emissions, Moderation, and Steeves’ Response

Covenant AI also alleged that its subnet was targeted via **suspended emissions**, **reduced moderation access**, and **indirect economic pressure** (e.g. token sales). **Steeves denied** the claim that decision power is concentrated in his hands alone, and stated that he **does not have authority to halt emissions** and operates under the **same permissions as other TAO holders**. He attributed emission changes to **normal market dynamics** tied to buying and selling, and said **moderation restrictions were temporary**.

## Market Reaction (as reported)

Reporting tied to the controversy cited TAO trading near **~$262** after roughly an **18.7%** drop over **24 hours**, with price previously above **~$320**, a sharp move through **~$290** and **~$270**, and a low near the **~$250** area before a weak recovery. *(Figures and sourcing—e.g. CoinCodex—reflect the article’s snapshot in time; verify live markets separately.)*
